# Brand Bootstrap

Use this guide to define your brand from scratch. Work through each section in order — each step builds on the previous one. By the end, you will have filled out the mission, marketing, and app store documents in this folder.

## Step 1: The Elevator Pitch

Start here. Answer these questions in plain language, as if explaining to a friend:

- What does your app do?
- Who is it for?
- Why would someone care?

Write a 2-3 sentence summary that ties these together. This becomes the foundation for everything else.

## Step 2: Mission (mission.md)

Now open `mission.md` and fill it out using your elevator pitch as a starting point.

1. **Company Mission** - Start broad. What is the company behind this app trying to do in the world? This should be bigger than any single product.
2. **Project Mission** - Narrow the focus. How does this specific app serve the company mission?
3. **Core Values** - Pick 3-5 principles that will guide decisions when trade-offs arise. These should be opinionated, not generic. "Fast over perfect" says something. "Quality" does not.
4. **Problem Statement** - Describe the pain point your users experience today. Be concrete. What are they doing instead? Why is it frustrating?
5. **Vision** - Paint a picture of what the world looks like when this app succeeds. Think 1 year and 3 years out.

## Step 3: Marketing (marketing.md)

With your mission clear, shift to who you are reaching and how.

1. **Target Audience** - Describe your primary audience as a specific person, not a demographic range. Give them a name if it helps. What do they care about? Where do they spend time online? Then do the same for a secondary audience if one exists.
2. **Positioning** - Look at what already exists in this space. What are people using today? Write one sentence that explains why your app is different — not better, different.
3. **Marketing Channels** - Be realistic about where your audience actually is. Pick 2-3 channels you can sustain, not 10 you will abandon. Think about organic reach first, paid second.
4. **Key Messaging** - Write 3-5 short statements that capture what your app is about. These should feel natural enough to say out loud. They will show up in ads, social posts, and the app store listing.

## Step 4: App Store Listing (app-store.md)

This is where your brand meets the public. Everything from the previous steps feeds into this.

1. **App Name** - Keep it short and memorable. Max 30 characters.
2. **Subtitle / Short Description** - Distill your elevator pitch into one punchy line.
3. **Long Description** - Structure it as:
   - Opening hook (1-2 sentences from your key messaging)
   - Core features as benefits, not technical specs
   - Social proof or credibility if you have it
   - Call to action
4. **Keywords** - Think about what your target audience would search for, not what you would search for. Use all 100 characters.
5. **Categories** - Pick the category where your competitors live, not the one where you wish you lived.

## Tips

- Be specific over being broad. "Meal planning for busy parents" beats "food and lifestyle app."
- Write for people, not algorithms. If the description sounds robotic, rewrite it.
- Revisit these documents as the product evolves. They are living documents, not homework assignments.
- When in doubt, return to the problem statement. Every brand decision should trace back to the problem you are solving.
